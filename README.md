## What does it do?

Every World Cup match gets a full probability breakdown. Win, draw, or loss. Over 2.5 goals. Both teams to score. Exact scores. The model estimates the true chance of each outcome, then compares it to what bookmakers are offering — so you can spot when a price looks too long.

During live matches, a real-time swing chart tracks the model's win probability minute by minute. Goals push it, red cards crash it. You can save any moment as a shareable snapshot.

The model also grades its own accuracy in public. Every prediction is locked before kickoff and scored after the final whistle.

## What makes it different?

**Honest track record.** Every pre-kickoff probability is locked in a snapshot, then compared against the actual result. The report card shows hit rate, Brier score, and how we compare to the market — good or bad, no cherry-picking.

**Correlation-aware multis.** Most sites multiply independent leg probabilities to price a multi. That's wrong when legs are in the same match — a favourite winning correlates with over 2.5 goals, but suppresses both-teams-to-score. The model reads the true joint probability off its score grid, so the fair price reflects the real correlation.

**Scoreboard vs the market and Opta.** Per-match head-to-head with the de-vigged bookmaker consensus, plus a 48-team tournament-level comparison against the Opta supercomputer — including where we agree and where we firmly disagree.
