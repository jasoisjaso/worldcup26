# Paper test v2: "Backing X" cards for Scotland v Brazil

Real numbers from the live M051 prediction at deploy `673e513` on
2026-06-24, kickoff 22:00 UTC. v2 voice pass after researching:

- **Pinnacle Betting Resources tone** (1,000+ articles, calm declarative,
  "process not picks", statistical language naturally, no hype words).
- **Marko, Thomas, Pitt & Daube 2023** (Frontiers in Sociology, on
  gambling-harm framing): personal-responsibility slogans like "treat
  it as entertainment", "when the fun stops, stop", "have a game plan"
  are evidence-free industry slogans that stigmatise. Don't preach.
- **CTA UX writing rules**: low-pressure, task-focused, no demands.

## Voice rules (v2)

1. **State the math, the reader decides.** Never "we recommend", "you
   should", "your best bet". The model's number is the recommendation.
2. **Numbers do the talking.** "Model 40%, bookies imply 27%. 13-point
   gap." No "huge edge", "real value", "meaningful gap" filler.
3. **No moralising.** Drop "treat it as the price of a beer", "save
   the stake for a real bet", "next month's rent". Stake sizes go in
   percent of bankroll, dry, factual.
4. **No fake intimacy.** No "mate", "lads", "you'll love this".
   Pinnacle voice is calm and dry.
5. **Take-action lines are task-focused, not demanding.** "Take at
   $3.00 or longer" not "Bet now at $3.00!"
6. **Card structure is the same every time.** Each card is exactly:
   one sentence framing the market, one paragraph with the numbers,
   one line with the stake and price floor.

## BACKING BRAZIL

### Story line

Brazil are favoured but the model sees a grind, not a goalfest.
Expected goals: 1.4 to Brazil, 0.5 to Scotland. Most likely score
is 0-1 Brazil (21%). The edge is in markets that pay for "quiet
Brazil win", not "Brazil run riot".

### Card 1: The straight back

> **Brazil to win straight, no clear edge.**
>
> Bookies pay $1.33 (imply 75%). Model has Brazil at 60%. The
> market is sharper than the model here, so a straight Brazil bet
> is roughly flat to slightly negative expected value.
>
> No model-suggested stake on this market.

### Card 2: The smarter bet

> **Brazil to win + under 2.5 goals.**
>
> Reading off the score grid, Brazil winning 1-0, 2-0 or 2-1
> happens about 36% of the time. Most bet-builders price this
> around $3.00, implying 33%. Three-point gap.
>
> Quarter-Kelly: 1.5% of bankroll. Take at $2.80 or longer.

### Card 3: The cleaner alternative

> **Brazil to win to nil.**
>
> Score grid: Brazil winning with Scotland scoring zero happens
> 48% of the time. Typical bookmaker price $2.20, implying 45%.
> Three-point gap, same shape as Card 2 but simpler to track.
>
> Quarter-Kelly: 1.8% of bankroll. Take at $2.10 or longer.

## Read aloud test

For each card, read it as if you're explaining the bet to a friend
who knows football but does not bet much. Three checks:

1. Does it state a fact, or does it tell them what to do?
   (We want fact. The numbers are the recommendation.)
2. Is there any phrase a tipster ad would use? Specifically:
   "huge edge", "smashing value", "the lock", "trust me", "you'll
   love this". (If yes, kill it.)
3. Does the stake line feel like an instruction, or like a number
   on a calculator? (We want calculator-output feeling.)

If all three check out across all six cards, the voice ships.

## What changed from v1

| v1 | v2 | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Strong back Brazil, model has them shorter than fair" | "Brazil to win straight, no clear edge" | v1 was wrong on this match anyway, but the v2 frame removes the "strong back" hype word |
| "Stake $5 on a $1,000 bankroll. Treat as the price of a beer, not next month's rent" | "No model-suggested stake on this market" | The beer/rent line is exactly the moralising RG slogan the research flagged. Skip the heart-bet permission entirely |
| "Worth a real bet" | (removed) | Filler. Numbers say the same thing |
| "Strong edge", "real value" | (removed) | Hype words |
| "Stake $20 on a $1,000 bankroll" | "Quarter-Kelly: 2.5% of bankroll" | Percent-of-bankroll is what the maths actually outputs. Dollar conversion is the user's calculator step, not ours. Less prescriptive |
| "Take any price $3.30 or longer" | "Take at $3.30 or longer" | Same content, lower-pressure phrasing |
| Three cards per side, third was "cover bet" or "hedge" | Third is now "cleaner alternative" with same shape as Card 2 but simpler markets | Most casual punters don't know what laying is. Cover-bet card was a knowledge cliff. Replaced with a parallel simpler market |
